OFFICERS

 

Salvatore Cicciarella (President)

Somers,CT 06071

 

Phone: 860-749-7693 (H), 860-729-4410 (C)

Email: scicciarella@cox.net

Biography:  Sal Cicciarella, retired Director of Choral Activities at Ellington High School, Ellington, CT. is very active as a guest conductor, clinician, and vocal adjudicator and served as conductor of the Vernon Chorale, Vernon CT. He holds degrees from the Boston Conservatory of Music, Eastern Connecticut University and has done extensive advanced degree work at the University of Hartford, Hartt School of Music where he conducts the Connecticut Men’s Choral Ensemble for the Hartt School Community Division, an auditioned honor male ensemble for high school men. Under his direction, the CT Men’s Ensemble was selected to perform at the Massachusetts and Connecticut All State Music Conferences. In the spring the ensemble was awarded a Superior Medal at Riverside Church in New York City with Heritage World Strides Music Festivals and has been invited to sing at Carnegie Hall. He has served in professional organizations including serving on the boards of the Connecticut Music Educators Association, past president of the American Choral Directors Association Connecticut Chapter. Mr. Cicciarella was voted by his peers as the recipient of the ACDA CT Chapter Choral Director of the Year Award and selected as the Connecticut’s Secondary Music Educator of the Year for the NAfME Connecticut Chapter. He has been invited to serve on the ACDA Eastern Division Executive Board for 16 years and presently holds the position of ACDA Eastern Division Repertoire Session Chair for the Eastern Division 2014 Conference in Baltimore, MD. Mr. Cicciarella is immediate past National Chair for the American Choral Directors Association for the Senior High Repertoire and Standards Committee and is President of The New England Music Festival Association, founded 87 years ago priding themselves with offering the finest adjudication process and festival experience for students in three honors ensembles. He continues his work in mentoring young music educators and is currently engaged as clinician, conductor, adjudicator, and leader of teacher development programs and honor performance ensembles in Connecticut, Rhode Island, Massachusetts, Vermont, New Jersey, Missouri, and Pennsylvania.
