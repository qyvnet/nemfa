Solo/Ensemble Festival Procedures

Selection of piece

Students may perform any piece as a solo or in any small ensemble for adjudication (verbal feedback and written evaluation). HOWEVER, if they wish to receive a numerical rating AND be considered for the New England Concert Festival ensembles, they MUST perform a piece from the NEMFA Repertoire listing. As of 2006, selections from the NYSSMA Manual are no longer allowed for consideration for Concert Festival acceptance eligibility.

Students are not allowed to perform the same piece they performed in any previous Solo & Ensemble Festival, regardless of whether or not they were accepted to the Concert Festival, if they wish to be considered for the Concert Festival.

In addition to solo listings for the four mature voice parts and all band and orchestral instruments (including Harp and Piano), the Association allows the following vocal ensembles: SSA, SSAA, SAB, SATB, SSATB, TTBB.

The publication listed on the NEMFA Repertoire for any piece is strongly suggested, but not required.

Again, performances which fall outside these guidelines are permissible, but will receive adjudicator comments only and will not eligible for Concert Festival selection.

Internet Produced Music / Public Domain

Any music produced from the Internet must also follow US copyright laws. If a student is performing a piece that came from an Internet sheet music or public domain music site, they must provide the documentation from that site noting its compliance with copyright laws. Pieces from such sites without this documentation will be considered illegal copies and the student performance will be disqualified from consideration for the Concert Festival.

Performance Adjudications

Each solo adjudication shall be scheduled for ten minutes with each vocal ensemble scheduled for 12 minutes. Each adjudication will consist of a complete, uninterrupted performance of music of the student's choice.

Some pieces on the listing are longer than 10 minutes, but still require a complete performance. UNLESS specifically requested on the listing, cadenzas are not performed as part of any piece.

Piano accompaniment for music so published is optional, but preferred. Music designated as unaccompanied must be performed thus. Prerecorded accompaniments may be utilized whenever live accompaniment is impossible or impractical, with the responsibility for supplying playback equipment resting with the applicant. The Association will provide and thereby reserves the right to use adequate electronic keyboards when deemed advisable.

The Association adheres to all current copyright laws, especially where it concerns the use of illegal copies of music. During the adjudication, students must perform from legally published, original music or from memory. Accompanists must perform from original, uncopied music as well. Where there is a bad page turn, it is permissible to use a facsimile of that page. Each performing student or ensemble at the Solo and Ensemble Festival shall provide a copy of the music to the adjudicator, preferably an original. However, under the educational use clause of the copyright laws, a copy may be provided, but it will be collected at the end of a school's adjudication(s) and destroyed by the adjudicator.

All students are expected to observe the following dress code. Girls: Dresses, blouses (no bare midriffs) and skirts, dress slacks, or school uniforms. No jeans. Boys: Dress slacks with shirt and tie, suits, jacket/sport coat with shirt and tie or turtleneck, or school uniform. No jeans. Athletic shoes are not acceptable.

Adjudication performances may be attended by any interested persons. Because it is a performance festival, it is acceptable for any interested person, including supervisors and students to audience performances at the Solo and Ensemble Festival. Audience members must conduct themselves appropriately at all times. They shall not distract the performer(s) and/or the adjudicator and, under no circumstances, are they permitted to interrupt a performance by leaving early or arriving late.

Current rules regarding registration numbers and types of performances shall be enforced.

Adjudicators of the highest quality available will be employed, and in the interest of fairness and consistency, every effort will be made to utilize the same adjudicators at the two solo and ensemble sites.

Copies of adjudicators' scores and critical comments will be made available online to supervisors as soon as possible after the Solo and Ensemble Festival.

Acceptance into the Concert Festival organizations will be determined by the Managers, their committees and members of the Executive Board. Acceptance is based upon adjudication scores from the Solo and Ensemble Festival and any other criteria currently in effect.

Copies of the adjudication forms used are available from the Solo & Ensemble section of the NEMFA website.

Instrumental Ensembles

Students may also perform in instrumental ensembles for an adjudicator BUT they will not receive any ratings and will NOT be considered for the Concert Festival based on that performance.

The ensemble may be of any configuration and perform any appropriate repertoire. The performance will receive adjudicator comments and feedback only.

Cadenzas

Students should not perform the cadenzas that are included in their performance pieces UNLESS specifically specified in the instructions on the NEMFA listing for that piece.

Accompaniment

Students are encouraged to perform their performance piece accompanied if it is written that way. A piano accompaniment makes a wonderful musical presentation for the adjudicator. Please indicate your accompanist on the application so that conflicting schedules can be avoided.

If a pianist is not available, then the use of taped or CD accompaniments is acceptable at the Solo and Ensemble Festival. However, the performer is responsible for providing his/her own tape or CD player. The Association does not provide either the recordings nor the equipment.

Students using SmartMusic (or its competitors) for accompaniment must provide the computer equipment (including speakers) to provide this playback. Internet access for student computers is not available at the Solo & Ensemble sites, so such accompaniment must come from files residing on the student provided computer.

Audiences

Audiences

It is acceptable for any interested person, including sponsors and students, to listen to any of the performances at the Solo and Ensemble Festival because it is a PERFORMANCE FESTIVAL. Audience members must conduct themselves appropriately at all times. Please do not disturb the performer(s) and/or the adjudicator and definitely DO NOT interrupt any performance by leaving early or arriving late. sponsors: BE SURE THAT YOUR STUDENTS ARE AWARE OF THIS!

Original Music

Again, as per copyright law, sponsors and students are reminded that performers (whether student or accompanist) can only perform from original pieces of music or from memory. Copies are not permitted at the Solo and Ensemble Festival except to facilitate an accompanist's or performer's difficult page turn and as an educational reference for the adjudicator.

Group Preferences

Please be consistent with your student's Group Preferences. Regardless of how many times or in how many ways, a student performs in the Solo & Ensemble Festival, their Group Preference should be the same in each application. No student who received a rating high enough to be accepted to the Concert Festival is ever refused that acceptance because of their Group Preference choice.

Student preferences are used to help place as many students in the group of their choice as possible. However, the overall educational and musical success of the Concert Festival is the primary function of the selection process and therefore takes precedence over student choices. NEMFA is not obligated to honor any preferences.

Students will only be accepted on the instrument or voice part on which they performed and received a rating high enough to warrant being invited to the Concert Festival.

Errors in applications

PRIOR TO OCTOBER 28th: Should you find that you made an error in your application data, return to the website, enter your NEMFA ID and edit your entries as needed. That data remains online and active until then.

AFTER OCTOBER 28th: The applications will be closed on that date, but the site will remain available for invoice and list printouts until November 5th. Should you find that you made an error in your application data after that date, please contact Steffen Parker immediately to see what rectification can be made.

If that error is an omission of a student or ensemble, please contact Steffen Parker to see if an addition can be made. Such additions incur an additional $20 per student fee over the current adjudication fee.

If the addition is not made until the day of the respective Solo & Ensemble Festival, there is an additional $50 per student per performance fee.

Schedule Changes or Additions

Requests for scheduling time considerations for all applicants from one school or sponsor are included in the application process as part of the School Information pages. Should you find that an individual student needs a different time than they were assigned after you have received the schedule, the sponsor has 10 days from the time the schedule is posted to request those changes by contacting Steffen Parker. After that, no schedule changes will be made.

Additions to your application can be accepted up until your S&E Saturday. Additions after the deadline incur an additional $20 fee. OnSite Applications will be accepted at each S&E site incurring a $50 additional fee.

Errors in adjudication forms

The adjudication forms with the scores and adjudicator's comments will be available online to the sponsors soon after the Southern Solo & Ensemble Festival's conclusion. Due to that major effort on your behalf, it is possible that an error slips through. Sponsors are requested to double-check all of their documentation for errors. If you find an error or have any concerns, email Steffen Parker IMMEDIATELY!

Weather

Due the large number of people involved, the complexity of the preparation and operation, the tightness of the schedule and the time of year they occur, the Solo and Ensemble Festivals are not cancelled due to weather. If it's bad out, take your time, drive carefully and your students will be heard when they arrive, even if you have missed your scheduled timeslots.

Questions

If you have any questions regarding the Solo and Ensemble Festival, do not hesitate to ask! Contact Steffen Parker via email (sparker@vpaonline.org) or via cellphone at 802.343.6282.
