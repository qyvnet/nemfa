Biography: Laura J. Hilton is the Region #4 Fine Arts Department Chair for the towns of Chester, Deep River, and Essex, CT as well as Valley Regional High School’s Choral Director. She has taught in the Region #4 Schools for 20 years offering classes in AP Music Theory, Vocal Technique, Music History and Musical Theater. She also conducts the Concert Choir, Women’s Choir, Madrigal Consort and "Madmen" vocal ensembles. Under her leadership, her ensembles have received Superior"Gold" Festival Ratings throughout the Eastern US. When not conducting, Miss Hilton performs trumpet professionally with the Norwich Concert Band, the Silver Cornet Band, The Ivoryton Playhouse, and other venues throughout CT. Outside of the US, Miss Hilton has performed both vocally and instrumentally in Holland, Switzerland, France, Belgium, Austria, and Germany. Prior to coming to Valley Regional High School, Miss Hilton taught band for 3 years in Old Lyme Middle School in Old Lyme, CT and 1 year in the Glastonbury Public Schools in Glastonbury, CT. Miss Hilton holds degrees from The Hartt School of Music in West Hartford, CT, Central CT State University, and a sixth year certificate from The University of Connecticut NEAG School of Education. She is a certified cooperating teacher for Connecticut’s TEAM Program, serving as a mentor to beginning educators. Miss Hilton has been very active in the Connecticut Music Educators Association, serving often as band and choral chair for the Southern Region Music Festival. Miss Hilton was involved in the NEMFA program as a student at East Lyme High School, East Lyme, CT (1979-1983) and as a teacher 1987 to present. In 2007-2011, Miss Hilton served as the Choral manager for the New England Music Festival and beginning in 2011, she will be the NEMFA President-Elect.
